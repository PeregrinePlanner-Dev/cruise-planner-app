💡 Smart Money Tips for Cruisers
✅ Notify your bank and credit cards before you go. Card companies often flag foreign charges as fraud and will decline your card mid-trip. Call or log in to set a travel notice for every card you plan to use — including the one you keep as a backup.
🏧 Best rate: local ATM at your destination. You'll typically get the interbank rate minus a small ATM fee — far better than airport kiosks or ship exchanges. Use a bank that reimburses ATM fees (Charles Schwab, Fidelity, some credit unions) and you're getting near-perfect rates.
💳 Use a no-foreign-transaction-fee card for purchases. Most major travel cards (Chase Sapphire, Amex Platinum, Capital One Venture) waive the 1–3% foreign transaction fee that regular cards charge. On a two-week trip, that fee adds up fast.
🚢 Avoid exchanging money on the ship. Cruise ship currency exchange desks offer some of the worst rates you'll find. Exchange before you board or use an ATM ashore.
✈️ Airport kiosks and hotel desks are nearly as bad. If you need cash before departure, order foreign currency through your bank in advance — most offer home delivery or in-branch pickup at competitive rates.
💵 USD is widely accepted in many cruise ports. Throughout the Caribbean, Bahamas, and most Mexican ports, US dollars are taken everywhere and change is often given in USD. You may not need local currency at all for a short port day.
⚠️ Watch Out: Dynamic Currency Conversion (DCC)
When you use your card at a foreign ATM or merchant, you may be asked "Would you like to pay in US Dollars?" — this is called Dynamic Currency Conversion and it is a trap. The merchant sets the exchange rate (often 3–8% worse than your bank's rate) and pockets the difference. Always choose to pay in the local currency. Your bank will give you a better rate every time.
